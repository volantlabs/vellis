A.3.7   Timing for behaviors, Decisions and merges

Decisions and merges are steps that enable sequences to be selected during execution, rather than ahead of time in
models, as in A.3.6 :

       •  Decisions are steps with multiple outgoing successions, but only one is traversed during each execution of
          the decision.
       •  Merges are steps with multiple incoming successions, but only one is traversed during each execution of
          the merge.

These are modeled by:

       •  Optional connector end multiplicities ([0..1]) on ends of the outgoing and incoming successions opposite
          decision and merge steps, respectively. This enables execution to determine which succession is traversed
          for each decision and merge.
